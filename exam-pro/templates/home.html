How the AI Algorithm Works
🧩
CSP Formulation
Each student is a variable. Seats are the domain. Constraints ensure no two students from the same class sit adjacent in any of 8 directions.
Variables · Domains · Constraints
🎯
MRV Heuristic
Minimum Remaining Values — assign the most constrained student first. This dramatically reduces the search space and speeds up solving.
Most Constrained Variable
⚖️
LCV Heuristic
Least Constraining Value — choose the seat that eliminates the fewest options for other students, keeping future assignments flexible.
Least Constraining Value
🔍
Forward Checking
After each assignment, immediately prune domains of neighboring seats. If any student loses all valid seats, backtrack early before wasting time.
Early Failure Detection
↩️
Backtracking Search
When a dead-end is reached, systematically undo assignments and try alternatives. Combined with heuristics, this finds solutions efficiently.
Systematic Exploration
🏛️
Multi-Room Distribution
Students are intelligently spread across 30 rooms. Same-class students go to different rooms first, then rooms fill up — minimizing intra-room conflicts.
Smart Distribution
